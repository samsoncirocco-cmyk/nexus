# Life is Fucking Hard

## The Concept

A book about the stuff nobody tells you. Not a self-help book full of toxic positivity and "just manifest it" garbage. An honest look at why life is hard, why that's okay, and how to keep going anyway.

## Why This Book

Because every bookshelf is full of two extremes:
1. **The hustle porn** - "Wake up at 4am, cold plunge, 10x your income"
2. **The therapy-speak** - "Honor your feelings, set boundaries, practice self-care"

Neither one captures what it actually feels like to be a real person dealing with real shit. Bills. Relationships. Career pressure. The gap between where you are and where you thought you'd be.

This book lives in the honest middle ground.

## Themes

- **The myth of having it figured out** - Nobody does. Stop comparing your behind-the-scenes to everyone else's highlight reel.
- **Work and identity** - When your job is more than a paycheck but less than your whole identity
- **Relationships are hard** - All of them. Family, romantic, friendships. The good ones are worth the work.
- **Money stress** - The thing everyone feels and nobody talks about honestly
- **Finding meaning** - Not in some grand cosmic sense, but in the daily grind
- **Resilience isn't pretty** - It's not motivational posters. It's getting up after getting knocked down, again.
